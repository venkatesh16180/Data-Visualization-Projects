# Project 2 — Data Visualization Essentials (IShowcase Poster)
**Course: INFO 526 | Team: InfoCrew | University of Arizona**

## 📌 Overview
A comprehensive data visualization cheat sheet designed to educate 
research students without a data visualization background on how to 
select appropriate chart types for their data. Presented as a poster 
at IShowcase (December 11, 2024) to 260+ graduate, PhD, and 
non-technical research attendees.

## 🔍 Research Questions
1. What role does color play in data visualization and how can it 
   be used effectively including for colorblind audiences?
2. How can visualizations help in interpreting complex datasets — 
   illustrated with real examples and comparison of similar chart types?

## 🔑 Key Topics Covered
- 6 major visualization types with real-world use cases
- Color-blind accessible design (Deuteranopia/Tritanopia palettes, 
  shape encoding, Okabe-Ito color scheme)
- Chart comparison framework: Bar vs Histogram, Jitter vs Strip, 
  Stacked Area vs Stacked Bar
- Implementations in both Python (Matplotlib, Seaborn) and R (ggplot2)
